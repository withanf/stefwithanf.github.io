Background and Goals

How can Georgia unite its residents to defeat COVID-19?

In March 2021, the COVID-19 vaccine became available to everyone 16 years or older in the United States, yet the sentiment of its effectiveness and safety were (and to an extent still are) polarizing and politically driven. In the state of Georgia,

I provided visual and responsive web design using DPH branding guidelines, working in tandem with a content strategist for copywriting. Over two weeks, we sought to drive cross-channel execution reaching GA residents where they were, reinforce messaging, and tell a unified story. Educate and counter misinformation. Concepts must speak to the general population and be customizable for spec8ific populations and geographies.

Research

Georgia resident survey data determined the following:

Manage expectations of vaccine availability and eligibility Reach vaccine-hesitant residents to educate and counter misinformation Once vaccines are more broadly available, drive broad community participation and vaccine uptake Campaign should be insight-based and address GA-specific attitudes and concerns Concepts must speak to the general population AND be easily customized for specific populations/geographies Ensure alignment with overall DPH look and feel and current/former messaging

Willingness to get the vaccine stats, 46% of surveyed residents believed the vaccine would be effective. Concerns surrounded side effects, not enough testing, and ineffectiveness. Here, include the pie chart and effectiveness bar chart.

Resident Data Willingness to receive vaccine: 44% willing, 34% not willing, 22% neutral 55% of all residents 55 years or older were willing 47% of Black residents were not willing, and 55% of Black residents aged 18-34 were not willing 44% of all residents aged 18-34 were not willing to get the vaccine Believing the vaccine wouldn’t be effective: 15% White residents, 28% Hispanic, 28% Black 40% of middle aged and 37% of aged 55+ Black residents were neutral about vaccine effectiveness Of people unwilling to get vaccine, residents predominantly concerned with vaccine side effects or insufficient testing 75% of those neutral to the vaccine said proven effectiveness would convince them

Design

The brand message: Band Together.

Recommendations Develop targeted messaging and campaigns to increase vaccine confidence for the Black community and younger demographics Emphasize the efficacy of the vaccine in targeted campaigns for racial and ethnic minority groups and younger demographics Reinforce through messaging that side effects from vaccine are normal, mild, and temporary Include messaging around rigorous testing, minimal side effects, there’s no virus in the vaccine, and vaccine efficacy Increase confidence in the vaccine by incorporating messaging about protecting loved ones, such as family and friends

Impact

Oh my god even more things. Why so many things? Just one more section bro it will work this time I swear bro just one more page section

Per the CDC as of July 2024, Georgia residents are getting the vaccine more and feel better about it. Black and Hispanic residents are also more confident in the safety and effectiveness of the COVID vaccine above the average for all adult residents.
