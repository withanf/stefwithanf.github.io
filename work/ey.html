Background

When companies transitioned to remote work in 2020, demand grew for virtual solutions to connect employees and impress customers. EY saw an opportunity for new work, and I saw an opportunity to learn new skills and mentor junior designers. I volunteered to help with this project and led the design direction, built high-level 3D objects, and ran testing. Two other designers assisted with 3D details, renders, and worked with the Unity developers to translate the model into the final product.

EY Wavespace CEC, Chicago, IL

Ideation

EY Metaverse aims to design and implement experience for client executive conversations and demonstrate technology enablers. We started by recreating the EY Cloud Enablement Center (CEC) in Phoenix, Arizona, where most of the team worked.

Prototyping

Moodboarding the interior design I proposed enhancing the user experience by incorporating large video screens, improving the view of the landscape, and creating a futuristic "command center" in the virtual space. Our design drew inspiration from the Arizona landscape, using clay, sandy neutrals, and succulents, while also introducing futuristic elements such as chrome and sci-fi shapes. To optimize navigation in VR, we replaced complex furniture with user-friendly, intuitively interactive, and performance-efficient objects, removing immersion-breakers like pantries, private office doors, and bathrooms. I began building out the floorplan in Blender and populating it with furniture assets. We rendered images of the space for feedback, which included requests for more chrome textures and to genericize the Arizona influence, essentially building a template for easy replication and modification.

After building in Blender, our team collaborated with Unity and VR developers to port the Blender files and conduct thorough testing, ensuring compatibility with various hardware and a comfortable user experience. The result exceeded expectations, offering features like interactive chairs, Surface Hubs with drawing tools, handheld objects, positional music, and live streaming PowerBI dashboards and MSNBC on the Command Center's walls.

Refinement

The team built virtual spaces for client workshops based on an initial template. The first was a virtual bank branch for Wells Fargo, which led to more projects from them. I facilitated this event, helping less tech-savvy folks strap on their headsets and teaching them controls. So far, these virtual spaces have been used to hold employee events and facilitate executive conversations. People can strap into VR headsets or log in from their computers, making it more accessible for smaller companies without a hardware budget and people with motion sickness. The tech has successfully sold to other clients across the finance, healthcare, tech, and insurance industries.

As part of the 2023 SXSW Art Program in Austin, TX, we collaborated with artist Josie Williams to showcase art that combines AI-driven experiences with Black culture and history. EY Learning Day Virtual Recruiting: We built a virtual office for campus hires to interact with onboarding materials at their own pace and chat live with the Recruiting team. Lessons Learned

I learned so much and I'm so cool and wow wow wow

More time building, less time planning. Much of what I put into the moodboard was changed over time. In the future, I’d be more scrappy with the initial work. Accessibility for VR. Being new to this space, I hadn’t accounted for accessibility concerns beyond those that also apply to web and mobile design. If I could do it over again, I’d push for more accessibility options, e.g., a desktop setup at the conference for people with motion sickness. Learn from the experts. There is a huge community out there for this, and if I’d spent more time within these communities, I likely would have picked up the 3D skills faster than from YouTube and Udemy alone.
